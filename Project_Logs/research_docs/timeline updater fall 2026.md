## Financial Aid and Next Steps

In general, **most scholarships require early applications or offers.** For example, UNSW’s Term-2 scholarships required an offer by Feb 26【121†L333-L342】, and Manchester’s global scholarship applications require holding an offer by Jan 14【118†L1-L7】. Late applicants often can only access automatically-awarded aid (e.g. merit reductions) or must rely on external funding. We recommend:

1. **Immediate Contact:** Email the international admissions or financial‐aid office at any still-open programs (e.g. Bristol, UNSW, Manchester) to ask about any remaining spots or scholarships for late applicants.

2. **Prepare Documents:** Gather transcripts, test scores, and references now. Even if deadlines seem tight, many rolling-intake programs will consider strong applicants on a space-available basis.

3. **Explore External Aid:** Look for government or private scholarships that accept applicants after admission (e.g. some Australian states have bursaries, or foreign-government scholarships to study abroad).

4. **Apply Promptly:** For the programs above with upcoming deadlines (Manchester Jun 30, UNSW May 14, ANU/USyd/UTS around May, HKUST Jun 30, etc.), submit applications ASAP to maximize chances of admission and any limited scholarships.

**Sources:** Official admissions and scholarship pages of each university (see citations above) provide the dates and policies. Each program’s international admissions office can give final confirmation of seats and aid availability.

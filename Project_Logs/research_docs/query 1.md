    - Non‑SDS applicants still need proof of the full duration of tuition plus living costs (IRCC’s benchmark ≈ CAD 10,000–12,000‑per‑year living costs, on top of tuition). [idp](https://www.idp.com/blog/visa-rules-for-international-students/)
  - Visa is usually **multi‑entry**, duration aligned with program length plus 90‑day grace. [idp](https://www.idp.com/blog/visa-rules-for-international-students/)

- **Post‑study work (PGWP 2026‑style):**  
  - PGWP: **1–3 years**, depending on program length; **3 years for master’s/PhD and some professional degrees** even if <2 years. [eecglobal](https://eecglobal.com/blog/canada-pr-after-study-2026)
  - PGWP is **open work permit**; you can work in any role/industry, but **must be on a Designated Learning Institution (DLI) program**; private “licensed” colleges are often excluded. [eecglobal](https://eecglobal.com/blog/canada-pr-after-study-2026)
  - Work hours during study: **24 hrs/week off‑campus**. [idp](https://www.idp.com/blog/visa-rules-for-international-students/)

- **PGWP → PR pathways (2026‑relevant):**  
  - Main routes:  
    - **Canadian Experience Class (CEC)** under Express Entry, using 1 year of Canadian skilled work (NOC 0, A, or B). [eduprofound](https://eduprofound.com/student-visa-to-pr-in-canada/)
    - **Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)** such as **OINP Masters Graduate** (no Canadian work‑exp needed for master’s grads in certain programs) or Employer‑driven streams. [eduprofound](https://eduprofound.com/student-visa-to-pr-in-canada/)
    - New one‑time **TR‑to‑PR pathway** for 33,000 temporary residents (launching 2026) can benefit some PGWP holders. [cicnews](https://www.cicnews.com/2026/03/canadas-new-tr-to-pr-pathway-has-launched-0372477.html)
  - **Rule of thumb timeline** (for CS‑AI grads):  
    - 2‑year diploma → 1‑year PGWP → 1–2 years work → PR ≈ **3–3.5 years total**. [eecglobal](https://eecglobal.com/blog/canada-pr-after-study-2026)
    - 1‑year master’s → 1‑year PGWP → 1–1.5 years work → PR ≈ **2.5–3 years**. [eduprofound](https://eduprofound.com/student-visa-to-pr-in-canada/)

**Key nit‑picks for you:**  
- Master’s is **structurally advantaged** (3‑year PGWP, spouse OWP, higher CRS points). [leapscholar](https://leapscholar.com/digest/top-10-best-destinations-indian-tech-graduates-2026)
- Avoid **1‑year diploma programs** if PR is the goal; 1‑year PGWP is too tight for full‑stack PR building. [karangupta](https://www.karangupta.com/blog/canada-pgwp-guide)

### **2. Germany: Zero‑tuition, strong job‑seeker, but finance‑blocked‑account–heavy**

- **Visa & finances (UG 2026):**  
  - **Student visa (§16b AufenthG):** must show **blocked account (Sperrkonto)** of **≈€11,208 per year** (≈€934/month) for living costs. [countrywidevisas](https://www.countrywidevisas.com/study/germany/pr-pathway)
  - In addition to tuition, you must show health insurance, proof of admission, and proof of language (often German B1/B2 for state universities, though English‑taught CS/AI programs may relax this). [yesgermany](https://www.yesgermany.com/post-study-work-visa-in-germany-pathways-to-pr-career-growth/)
  - Many public universities **charge no tuition** (only semester fees ≈ €150–300/semester). [leapscholar](https://leapscholar.com/digest/top-10-best-destinations-indian-tech-graduates-2026)

- **Post‑study work & job‑seeker options:**  
  - After graduation you can extend your residence permit to search for a job relevant to your degree (often formalised as a **job‑seeker period** or residence under §20). [countrywidevisas](https://www.countrywidevisas.com/study/germany/pr-pathway)
  - Typical **job‑seeker‑style stay** is **18 months**; you can work up to **120 full‑days or 240 half‑days per year** during this period. [yesgermany](https://www.yesgermany.com/post-study-work-visa-in-germany-pathways-to-pr-career-growth/)
  - Once you land a **qualified job**, you transition to a **work‑based residence permit** (often under §18b), then to **EU Blue Card** if salary thresholds are met (2026 Blue Card threshold ≈ €45,300–58,400 depending on shortage vs non‑shortage occupation). [yesgermany](https://www.yesgermany.com/pr-in-germany-after-study/)

- **PR and citizenship:**  
  - **PR (Niederlassungserlaubnis):** typically requires **5 years of continuous residence**, contributions to social security, stable income, B1/B2 German, and clean record. [yesgermany](https://www.yesgermany.com/pr-in-germany-after-study/)
  - **Blue Card faster track:** after **33 months** (if German B1), or **21 months** with **B2**. [countrywidevisas](https://www.countrywidevisas.com/study/germany/pr-pathway)
  - In practice, CS/AI grads in demand fields can land jobs in Germany within 6–12 months of graduation, then stack 5 years toward PR. [leapscholar](https://leapscholar.com/digest/top-10-best-destinations-indian-tech-graduates-2026)

**Nit‑picks for Indian UG CS/AI:**  
- **No tuition** at public universities is a **huge financial advantage** vs US/UK/Australia. [leapscholar](https://leapscholar.com/digest/top-10-best-destinations-indian-tech-graduates-2026)
- **German language B1/B2 is effectively mandatory** for long‑term PR and better job options; English‑only tracks are limited to big tech and R&D hubs. [yesgermany](https://www.yesgermany.com/pr-in-germany-after-study/)

### **3. Netherlands: “Zoekjaar,” flat‑tax, but salary‑sensitive PR**

- **PR pathway:**

  - Many CS/AI grads move from study visa → orientation year → HSM permit → permanent residence after 5 years. [kadamboverseas](https://kadamboverseas.com/post-study-work-visa-europe-2026-country-comparison-indian-students/)

**Nit‑picks:**

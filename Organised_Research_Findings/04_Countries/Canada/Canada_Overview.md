## 🛂 Visa & Immigration Status (Study Permit)
- **Status Emoji:** 🔴 **High Risk (Initial Entry) / 🟢 Easy (Post-Grad PR)**
- **Financial Proof:** **$22,895 GIC (Guaranteed Investment Certificate)** + First-year tuition.
- **Study Permit Cap:** Strictly limited to **155,000 new permits** globally for 2026.
- **The PAL System:** All undergraduate and professional graduate programs require a **Provincial Attestation Letter**. 
  - **Exemption:** Master's and PhD students are **exempt** from the cap and PAL.
- **Work Rights:**
  - **In-Study:** 24 hrs/week off-campus (increased from 20).
  - **Post-Study (PGWP):** 1–3 years. **Master's students automatically qualify for 3 years.**

## 🎯 Exam Relevance (Waterloo, UofT, UBC)

### SAT (🟡 Supplementary — Grades-First System)
- SAT is **generally not mandatory** for Canadian universities, even for international applicants.
- Waterloo places utmost emphasis on **90%+ in Calc/Physics** and contest scores.
- A submitted **1550+** (especially an 800 Math) verifies quantitative aptitude against a global benchmark, valuable if local grading system is prone to inflation.

### JEE (🟡 Rigor Signal — Contextual Value)
- Top JEE ranks are viewed as evidence of quantitative strength but do **not** bypass standard grade requirements.
- Not structurally recognized as a formal bypass mechanism in Canada.

### 🏆 Canadian Math Contests (🟢 THE Critical Differentiator for Waterloo CS)
- **Euclid Mathematics Contest:** The single most important supplementary credential for Waterloo CS. Top scores (85+/100) dramatically elevate applications.
- **CCC (Canadian Computing Competition):** Stage 1 and Stage 2 results are highly valued for CS/Software Engineering at Waterloo.
- These contests are **more important than SAT** for Canadian CS admissions.

| Exam / Contest | Value at Waterloo CS | Value at UofT/UBC |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Euclid Contest (85+)** | 🟢 **Critical Differentiator** | 🟡 Moderate |
| **CCC (Stage 2)** | 🟢 **Critical for CS/SE** | 🟡 Positive Signal |
| **SAT 1550+** | 🟡 Supplementary (if submitted) | 🟡 Supplementary |
| **JEE Advanced** | 🟡 Contextual rigor signal | 🟡 Contextual |

## 🔴 Policy Traps & Hostility Signals
1. **The 74% Refusal Trap:** Indian applicants facing a massive rejection spike (Geopolitical + Integrity checks).
2. **CIP Code Restriction:** PGWP eligibility for diplomas is now frozen and tied to 1,107 specific "labor shortage" fields. **Degree programs are exempt.**
3. **Diploma Shop Risk:** Avoid private "licensed" colleges; many have lost PGWP eligibility.

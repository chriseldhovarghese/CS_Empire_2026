# Utah State University (USU)

> **Location:** Logan, Utah, USA
> **Track:** Fall 2026 (rolling admissions available) / Fall 2027
> **Program:** BS in Computer Science / Computer Engineering

---

## 🔬 Core Technical Specializations

### Silicon / Chip Design — Aerospace & Defense Focus
- **Utah Nanofab** — 6,000 sq ft cleanroom with full semiconductor fabrication tooling.
- **UNICOS (Utah Network for Integrated Computing and Semiconductor Research)** — Under Director Hanseup Kim.
- **Strategic Focus:** Designing computer chips hardened for **harsh aerospace environments** — directly aligned with CHIPS Act domestic workforce mandates.
- **Key Insight:** USU sits at the intersection of **defense aerospace + microelectronics** — a niche no other target school occupies.

### Robotics
| Lab | Director | Focus |
|:----|:---------|:------|
| **ARIA Lab (Aligned, Robust, and Interactive Autonomy)** | — | Autonomous systems, robust AI decision-making |
| **Magnetic & Medical Robotics Lab** | Jake Abbott | Magnetic telemanipulation for non-invasive in vivo surgery |
| **Human-Centered Haptics** | Edoardo Battaglia | Wearable sensing, human-robot interaction |

- These labs are **deeply integrated** with both Mechanical Engineering and CS departments.
- **Unique niche:** Jake Abbott's **magnetic telemanipulation** of medical robots for non-invasive surgical procedures is globally rare at the undergraduate level.

### Bio-Computation — "Executable Biology"
- **Nicholas Flann** — Uses the Cellular Potts Model (CPM) to simulate living systems in 2D/3D. This is a fundamentally different approach: building computational models that simulate how subcellular regulatory networks control development and disease.
- **Lukas Buecherl** — Evolutionary AI algorithms optimizing synthetic genetic circuits — bridging EE, Python programming, and synthetic biology.
- **Key Insight:** USU's bio-comp approach is **computational modeling + synthetic biology**, not CRISPR-centric. This is a distinctive niche.

## 🎓 Undergraduate Research Architecture

| Program | Structure | Compensation | Access Level |
|:--------|:----------|:-------------|:-------------|
| **URCO (Undergraduate Research & Creative Opportunity)** | Grant-based | Funding for research + travel | Open application |
| **URF (Undergraduate Research Fellowship)** | Multi-year | $1,000/yr scholarship + travel + VIP cohort access | Available to **incoming freshmen** |

- **URF is the standout:** It provides **freshmen** with immediate research cohort placement, scholarship funding, and travel support from Day 1.
- This multi-year longitudinal model is similar to MIT's UROP in immediacy but with added financial incentive packaging.

## ⚠️ Strategic Notes

1. **Safety school with genuine niche depth.** USU is NOT a compromise — it has real, funded research infrastructure in aerospace chip design and medical robotics that many T50 schools lack.
2. **USU + CHIPS Act alignment** = strong federal funding pipeline. Undergrads working on hardened aerospace chips are directly employable by defense contractors (Northrop Grumman, Raytheon, L3Harris).
3. **Rolling admissions** make USU the optimal **Track A (Fall 2026)** backup — can apply late with minimal risk.
4. **URF from freshman year** ensures research access isn't delayed — critical for PhD pipeline planning.
5. **Cost-effective:** At $25-30K OOS, USU is 1/3 the cost of MIT/Stanford, making ROI exceptional if leveraging the niche labs.
